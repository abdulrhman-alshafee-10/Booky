A luminous debut about a lighthouse keeper's daughter who uncovers a century-old secret buried beneath the tides of Aldridge Bay. When Maren inherits the crumbling lighthouse after her father's disappearance, she finds his journals speak of a light that doesn't come from the lamp.

A sweeping story of memory, loss, and the light we leave behind — longlisted for the Booker Prize and shortlisted for the Costa First Novel Award.

"Finch writes with the precision of poetry and the warmth of an open fire. A debut for the ages." — The Guardian

About the author

Eleanor Finch was born in Cornwall and studied English Literature at Oxford. The Lantern of Aldridge Bay is her debut novel. She lives with her two cats and an unreasonable number of unread books.
