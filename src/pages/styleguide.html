What Makes a Great Literary Novel?

Literary fiction occupies a unique space in the world of books. Unlike genre fiction, which often prioritises plot and pace, literary fiction invests deeply in character, language, and meaning. But that doesn't make it inaccessible — the best literary novels are as gripping as any thriller.

"Not all those who wander are lost." — J.R.R. Tolkien

The Elements of Craft

Three things define great literary writing:

  • Precise, evocative language that earns its place on the page.
  • Characters that feel fully realised — contradictory, surprising, and human.
  • Themes that resonate beyond the narrative, touching on universal truths.

The best novels leave you changed. They offer a new lens through which to view the world, a new vocabulary for experiences you couldn't previously articulate.
