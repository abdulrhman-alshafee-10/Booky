Fiction Reading Life Staff Picks History Writing Non-Fiction

Books have a unique power: they can transport you across continents, reframe your assumptions, and leave you fundamentally different by the final page. This list isn't curated for novelty — it's curated for impact.

Why these books

The criteria were deceptively simple: did the book change the way you see something you thought you already understood? The answers surprised us. History books challenged how we think about the present. Novels illuminated truths that journalism couldn't.

"A book that changes you doesn't announce itself. You finish it and only realise, weeks later, that you're thinking differently." — Elena Marlow

Fiction that rewires your brain

The novels in this section aren't just good stories — they operate on multiple levels simultaneously. Reading them once is enough to be moved. Reading them again reveals an architecture you missed entirely.

On perception and reality

The first title explores how the stories we tell about ourselves become the stories we live by. It's formally experimental but never difficult, weaving three timelines together until the reader can no longer be sure which is which — which is precisely the point.

The greatest trick narrative plays is convincing you that you are outside it.

On grief and time

Grief literature can fall into predictable territory. This one does not. It is the most structurally inventive and emotionally precise account of loss we have encountered in years of editing.

Non-fiction that reads like fiction

The best narrative non-fiction collapses the gap between literary pleasure and factual rigour. These titles share a gift for revealing the extraordinary in what should, by rights, be ordinary.

Key takeaways

  • Narrative non-fiction benefits from close reading you'd bring to literary fiction.
  • The most illuminating books often concern systems, not individuals.
  • The most enduring titles tend to age into relevance rather than out of it.

How to read this list

Don't treat it as a curriculum. These books might be exactly right for where you are right now. We've kept descriptions brief deliberately. The less you know going in, the more the book can do its work.
