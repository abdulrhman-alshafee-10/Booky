Marina Vøllo

Marina Vøllo was born on a small island off the northern coast, where the tide sets the clock and the light changes by the hour. For thirty years she has written about distance — the kind the sea makes, and the kind we make ourselves.

Her eight books move between fiction and essay, but they share one preoccupation: how people stay close across great gaps of time and water. Salt & Starlight, her latest novel, follows a forty-year correspondence between two islands.
